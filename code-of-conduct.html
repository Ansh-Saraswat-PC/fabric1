Code of Conduct

This document sets forth the standards of professional conduct expected of all participants — members, speakers, organisers, and guests — of the Greater Noida Microsoft Fabric User Group. Adherence to these standards is a condition of participation in all community activities.

📅 Effective: September 2025 📍 Greater Noida Microsoft Fabric User Group
§
Section 1

Purpose & Scope

The Greater Noida Microsoft Fabric User Group ("the Group") is a professional community dedicated to the advancement of knowledge in Microsoft Fabric, Power BI, and modern data analytics. This Code of Conduct ("the Code") establishes the behavioural expectations and community standards applicable to all participants.

This Code applies across all Group-affiliated spaces and activities, including but not limited to: in-person meetups and workshops, official digital communication channels (WhatsApp, LinkedIn), this website, and any other platform or medium operated under the Group's name.

Participation in any Group activity — whether in person or online — constitutes implicit acceptance of this Code of Conduct in its entirety.
🤝
Section 2

Member Conduct Principles

All Group members are expected to uphold the following principles throughout each and every interaction within the community:

😊 Be Friendly and Welcoming
  • Listen with purpose and create space for others' communication preferences.
  • Ask yourself how you can make someone's life easier and act on it.
⏳ Be Patient
  • Remember that people have varying communication styles and preferences.
  • Recognise that not everyone is using their native language. Meaning and tone can be lost in translation.
💭 Be Thoughtful
  • Think about how others will interpret your words. Aim for clear and productive communication.
  • Remember that sometimes it is best to refrain from commenting.
🌐 Be Respectful and Inclusive
  • Respect differences of opinion.
  • Seek to understand and build bridges, not condemn or criticise.
  • Make a conscious effort to include people who differ from you.
🔍 Be Open and Curious
  • Assume good intent and interpret others' statements or questions in good faith.
  • Ask questions to understand, not to denounce.
  • Focus on continuous learning. Improve upon the things you already know, tackle new things, and ask others about their expertise to deepen your own knowledge.
⊞
Section 3

Microsoft Fabric User Group Code of Conduct

In addition to the member conduct principles outlined above, all participants also agree to abide by the official Microsoft Fabric User Group Code of Conduct. The following summary applies to all interactions within this Group:

  • ✓ Treat everyone with respect, kindness, and empathy.
  • ✓ Use welcoming and inclusive language at all times.
  • ✓ Be thoughtful in how you communicate — both in person and online.
  • ✓ Do not be destructive or inflammatory in any discussion or interaction.
  • ✓ Gracefully accept constructive criticism as an opportunity for growth.
  • ✓ Listen with purpose and create space for others' communication preferences.
  • ✓ Reach out to the organisers if you need anything or have a concern.
Reporting to Microsoft: Concerns, suspicious activity, or disruptive behaviour may also be reported directly to the Microsoft Fabric Community team at fbccommunity@microsoft.com.
🚫
Section 4

Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviours are strictly prohibited and constitute a violation of this Code, regardless of intent:

  • 4.1 Harassment. Any form of harassment — including verbal abuse, intimidation, deliberate exclusion, unwanted contact, or sustained disruptive behaviour — directed at any individual.
  • 4.2 Discriminatory Language or Behaviour. Use of language or conduct that demeans, marginalises, or discriminates against individuals on the basis of any personal characteristic including gender, race, religion, nationality, disability, or experience level.
  • 4.3 Unauthorised Commercial Activity. Unsolicited advertising, repeated self-promotion, or solicitation of business not sanctioned by Group leadership.
  • 4.4 Intellectual Property Violations. Presenting, distributing, or reproducing the work, code, or intellectual property of others without appropriate credit and explicit permission.
  • 4.5 Disruption of Events. Any conduct that materially disrupts Group events, sessions, or online activities, including repeated off-topic interventions or wilful interference.
  • 4.6 Misrepresentation. Misrepresenting one's professional credentials, affiliation with the Group, or the official positions of Group leadership.
⚙️
Section 5

Enforcement & Disciplinary Process

Group leadership is responsible for upholding this Code and will exercise fair and proportionate judgement in all enforcement matters. The disciplinary process shall generally proceed as follows, though leadership reserves the right to escalate directly in cases of severe violation:

1
Formal Written Warning A private written communication identifying the specific violation, its impact, and the required corrective behaviour. A record of the warning shall be retained by Group leadership.
2
Temporary Suspension Temporary exclusion from one or more Group spaces — including events, WhatsApp group, and online channels — for a defined period as determined by leadership.
3
Permanent Removal Permanent revocation of membership and exclusion from all Group activities, reserved for repeated violations or conduct of a particularly serious nature.
Note: All disciplinary decisions made by Group leadership are final. Participants subject to disciplinary action will be notified privately and afforded an opportunity to respond before a final determination is made, except where the nature of the violation necessitates immediate action.
📩
Section 6

Reporting a Violation

Any participant who witnesses or experiences conduct in violation of this Code is encouraged to report the incident promptly to Group leadership. All reports will be treated with the utmost confidentiality and investigated in a timely manner. Retaliation against any individual who, in good faith, reports a violation is itself a violation of this Code.

Reports may be submitted via direct email to Group leadership, or to Microsoft directly:

📝
Section 7

Amendments & Review

This Code of Conduct is subject to periodic review and amendment by Group leadership. Any material revisions will be communicated to the community via official channels no less than seven (7) days prior to taking effect. Continued participation following the effective date of any amendment shall constitute acceptance of the revised Code.

Members who wish to propose additions or modifications to this Code are encouraged to submit their recommendations in writing to Group leadership for consideration.
